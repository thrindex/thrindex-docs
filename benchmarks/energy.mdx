## Formula

```
energy_pJ = Σ_layer (pre_spikes_in_layer × fan_out_of_layer) × coefficient_pJ
```

| Symbol | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `pre_spikes_in_layer` | Spike events from the pre-synaptic population across all timesteps |
| `fan_out_of_layer` | Post-synaptic connections per neuron (= `out_features` for Dense, `out_channels × kH × kW` for Conv2d) |
| `coefficient_pJ` | Energy per synaptic operation (default: **0.5 pJ/syn-op**) |

The product `pre_spikes × fan_out` is the number of **synaptic operations** (multiply-accumulates triggered by spikes). This is the standard neuromorphic energy metric.

## Worked example

`Dense(700→512) → LIF → Dense(512→20) → LIF`, 100 timesteps, 10% spike rate:

```
Layer 1 (Dense 700→512):
  pre_spikes  = 700 × 100 × 0.10 = 7,000
  fan_out     = 512
  ops         = 7,000 × 512 = 3,584,000

Layer 3 (Dense 512→20):
  pre_spikes  = 512 × 100 × 0.10 = 5,120
  fan_out     = 20
  ops         = 5,120 × 20 = 102,400

Total ops   = 3,686,400
Energy      = 3,686,400 × 0.5 pJ/syn-op = 1.84 µJ
```

LIF layers have no synaptic weights and contribute no ops.

## The coefficient

**0.5 pJ/syn-op** is the default. It is consistent with published neuromorphic silicon figures from 7nm digital processes. It is not calibrated to any specific device or operating point.

To override: `thrindex run model.thx --energy-coeff 3.0` (units: pJ/syn-op).

## What the estimate captures and does not capture

**Captures:** the spike-driven efficiency advantage. Energy scales with spike count, not model parameter count. A feedforward ANN with the same parameter count processes all multiplications every timestep — roughly `1/spike_rate` more energy for the same architecture at 10% activity.

**Does not capture:** memory access energy, on-chip routing energy, chip idle power, host interface overhead, or system-level power.

**Not a silicon measurement.** For hardware-measured power, see the device datasheet for your target chip.
